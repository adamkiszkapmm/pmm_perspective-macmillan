Value propositions across university stakeholders

One product, four very different buyers

Each stakeholder group buys for a different reason, and needs a different message. Here’s how I think about positioning for each.

Buying: Institutional outcomes

Key factors

  • Student retention and graduate employability
  • Enrollment competitiveness
  • Institutional reputation
  • Peer institution proof points
Message: Prepare students for the workforce, not just graduation.
Buying: Academic trust and autonomy

Key factors

  • A skeptical buyer by default
  • Driven by peer recommendations and academic rigor
  • Needs to feel like a complement to their teaching, not an imposition on it
  • Credibility has to come from within their own community
Message: This complements what you already teach.
Buying: Risk reduction

Key factors

  • Integration, security, and LMS compatibility
  • Total cost of ownership
  • Support structure and SLAs
  • Risk management, not vision
Message: Works with your systems, won’t create problems.
Buying: Career readiness and employability

Key factors

  • Career-relevant curriculum and content
  • Tangible career outcomes
  • An engaged learning experience that prepares students for the workforce
  • Accessibility across print and digital formats
Message: Learn the skills employers are already asking for.
Key insight

The biggest GTM mistake in discipline vertical marketing is using the same message for all audiences. Faculty in Biology think differently than faculty in Economics.

How I orchestrate cross-functional GTM

Product Marketing as the connective tissue

Cross-functional alignment works best when everyone is bought into GTM goals before execution begins, not at the handoff. My framework: what we set out to accomplish, what we measured, what moved, and what to adjust next.

I managed GTM across Business, Math, and Science discipline verticals at Cengage, each with its own faculty culture, adoption cycle, and competitive dynamics. Early launches often surfaced clear needs for improvement: for A&P, the need for a lab manual to be fully competitive and additional digital support for physiology content; for Statistics, additional analytical features needed for the Statistical Analysis and Learning Tool (SALT) to be fully viable.
